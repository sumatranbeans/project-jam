### *"Think alike. Score big. Become telepathic."*

## The Hook

**Can you think like your family thinks?**

MIND MELD is simple: Everyone sees the same prompt. Everyone writes their answer secretly. Points are scored for *matching* other players. The goal isn't to be clever or uniqueâ€”it's to be *typical*, to think what you think others will think.

But here's the twist: families develop their own "mind melds" over time. Inside jokes emerge. Predictable patterns form. Suddenly, getting into each other's heads becomes the game.

---

## The Magic

The joy comes from the tension between "thinking for yourself" and "thinking like the group." When 4 out of 5 players all write "PIZZA" for "Best food ever" and one person writes "SUSHI"â€”that person becomes the lovable outlier, the family's beautiful weirdo.

---

## How It Works

### A Round:
1. **TV shows a prompt**: "An animal you'd find at the zoo"
2. **Everyone types their answer secretly on their phone**
3. **Timer: 10 seconds** (urgency is part of the fun)
4. **Reveal**: TV shows all answers simultaneously
5. **Scoring**: Points for each person who matched you

# ğŸ­ Game 3: REVERSE CHARADES

### *"One guesser. Everyone else acts. Pure chaos."*

---

## The Hook

**What if the WHOLE TEAM had to act out the same thing... at the same time?**

In regular Charades, one person acts while everyone guesses. In REVERSE CHARADES, ONE person closes their eyes while EVERYONE ELSE scrambles to act out the same word together. The chaos of multiple people attempting synchronized mime is inherently hilarious.

---

## The Magic

There's something uniquely joyful about watching 4-5 people try to act out "ELEPHANT" simultaneouslyâ€”some doing the trunk, some doing big ears, someone stomping, someone just standing there panicking. The guesser opens their eyes to pure delightful chaos.

---

## How It Works

### A Round:
1. **Guesser Selection**: One player is chosen (rotates each round)
2. **Eyes Closed**: Guesser covers eyes or turns around
3. **Word Display**: TV shows the word to all other players
4. **Countdown**: 3... 2... 1... ACT!
5. **The Performance**: All players act simultaneously for 30 seconds
6. **Guessing**: Guesser shouts out guesses while watching
7. **Point or Pass**: Correct guess = team point, time out = no point

# ğŸ¯ Game 8: FACE VALUE

### *"Make a face. We'll make it make sense."*

---

## The Hook

**Make an expression. Others write what caused it.**

One player makes a face (surprise, disgust, confusion, joy, etc.). Everyone else writes what they think CAUSED that face. The face-maker picks their favorite explanation. Simple. Endlessly hilarious.

---

## The Magic

The same confused face might get explanations ranging from "You just stepped in something wet while wearing socks" to "You discovered your dog knows how to use the TV remote." The creativity of the captions is the content.

---

## How It Works

### A Round:
1. **Face Assignment**: One player gets an emotion prompt (SURPRISED, DISGUSTED, etc.)
2. **The Performance**: They make that face and HOLD it for the room
3. **Caption Time**: Everyone else writes what caused that face (45 seconds)
4. **The Vote**: Face-maker picks their favorite caption
5. **Points**: Winner gets points; face-maker gets points for participation
