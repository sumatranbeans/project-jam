# Project Jam: Game Concepts
## Phase 1: Concept Development

*7 Original Multiplayer Family Games*

---

## Overview

This document presents 7 original game concepts developed for Project Jam. Each game is designed to:

- Work with the TV + phone setup (shared screen + individual devices)
- Be playable by ages 6+ through adults
- Complete in under 5 minutes
- Create memorable "magic moments"
- Teach something valuable while being genuinely fun

---

| # | Game | Type | Players | Learning Focus | Core Mechanic |
|---|------|------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Blurt!** | Word Game | 3-6 | Vocabulary, Quick Thinking | Describe without saying the word |

### How to Play

1. **Split into two teams** (or play individually with 3+ players)
2. **One player is the Describer** - they see a secret word and 6 forbidden words on their phone
3. **Describer talks!** - Explain the word to teammates using any words EXCEPT the forbidden ones
4. **Teammates shout guesses** - No phones needed, just listen and guess!
5. **Score points** - Each correct guess = 1 point. The Describer rotates each round.
6. **Watch the timer!** - You have 60 seconds to get as many words as possible

**The Catch:** Say a forbidden word? Hit the BUZZ button yourself (honor system!) or opponents can challenge. That word is skipped!

---

### Player Setup

| Setting | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Players | 3-6 (optimal: 4-6) |
| Teams | 2 teams, or free-for-all with rotating describer |
| Duration | 3-5 minutes (3 rounds per team) |
| Difficulty | Easy (3 forbidden) / Medium (5 forbidden) / Hard (7 forbidden) |

---

### What You'll Learn

| Skill | How It's Developed |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Vocabulary** | Finding synonyms and alternative descriptions |
| **Quick Thinking** | Processing language under pressure |
| **Communication** | Explaining concepts clearly and creatively |
| **Active Listening** | Teammates must listen carefully to guess |
| **Verbal Fluency** | Speaking smoothly while monitoring word choice |

---

### The Magic Moment

**The Hilarious Slip-Up:** The Describer is doing great, on a roll, then accidentally says a forbidden word and immediately gasps, slaps their forehead, and everyone erupts in laughter. "I said CHEESE! NOOO!"

**The Impossible Save:** With 5 seconds left, the Describer is struggling. They go completely abstract: "It's that thing you get when you're hungry and you call someone and they bring it to your house in 30 minutes!" "PIZZA!" Team erupts!

---

### Why It's Addictive

- **"I could've done better!"** - Players always feel they could explain words faster
- **Rotating roles** - Everyone gets to be the star (and the one who slips up)
- **Escalating difficulty** - Easy words give confidence, hard words create drama
- **Team investment** - You're cheering for your Describer, groaning at mistakes
- **Instant replay potential** - "Let me try that word again!"

- **Private info:** Only describer sees forbidden words on phone

- **Simultaneous play option:** Two describers, two guessers, race format

### How to Play

1. **Everyone draws!** - All players see the SAME word on their phones and have 20 seconds to draw it
2. **Drawings go to the TV** - One by one, each player's artwork appears on the big screen
3. **Guess on your phone** - When you see someone else's drawing, type your guess! (You can't guess your own)
4. **Speed matters!** - First to guess correctly gets 3 points, second gets 2, third gets 1
5. **The reveal** - After all guesses, the word is shown and everyone sees who got it right
6. **Rotate through all drawings** - Every player's art gets a moment to shine (or confuse)

**Scoring:**
- Guess correctly first: 3 pts
- Guess correctly second: 2 pts
- Guess correctly third: 1 pt
- If everyone guesses your drawing: Bonus 2 pts!

---

| Setting | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Players | 3-6 (optimal: 4-5) |
| Teams | Individual (everyone competes) |
| Duration | 3-4 minutes (5 rounds with 5 drawings each = 25 guesses total) |
| Difficulty | Auto-scales based on age selection |

---

### What You'll Learn

| Skill | How It's Developed |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Visual Communication** | Conveying ideas through simple images |
| **Quick Processing** | Rapidly interpreting abstract visuals |
| **Fine Motor Skills** | Drawing quickly on a touch screen |
| **Pattern Recognition** | Identifying objects from partial info |
| **Creative Problem-Solving** | How do you draw "happiness"? |

---

### The Magic Moment

**The Masterpiece-to-Mystery:** Dad spends 20 seconds on an incredibly detailed horse... and everyone guesses "dog," "cow," "dinosaur." The reveal that it was a horse makes everyone howl.

**The 3-Second Genius:** The 7-year-old draws two circles and a line. Within 2 seconds, everyone guesses "snowman." Sometimes simple wins!

**The Abstract Artist:** Someone's drawing of "birthday" is so creative (a calendar with a star!) that even though it's unconventional, it sparks a conversation about creative thinking.

---

### Why It's Addictive

- **No waiting!** - Everyone plays every round, no sitting out
- **Fail forward** - Bad drawings are funnier than good ones
- **Skill doesn't dominate** - Quick simple drawings often beat slow detailed ones
- **Competitive but kind** - You're judging drawings, not people
- **Rapid feedback** - Instant results, points flying, always moving

### How to Play

2. **First player gives a clue** - One word that relates to the START word

5. **Last player says the goal** - If the chain logically leads there, you win!
6. **Judge together** - Does the bridge make sense? Group votes to accept!
