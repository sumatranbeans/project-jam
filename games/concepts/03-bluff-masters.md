## How to Play

### Round Flow (2-3 minutes per question)

1. **The Question (5 sec)**
   - TV displays an interesting trivia question
   - Questions are designed to be obscure but guessable

2. **Bluff Writing (30-45 sec)**
   - Everyone writes a fake answer on their phone
   - Goal: Make it sound believable!
   - The TRUE answer is added to the mix automatically

3. **The Lineup (5 sec)**
   - All answers appear on TV (shuffled, anonymous)
   - Players read through the options

4. **Voting (20 sec)**
   - Everyone votes for which answer they think is TRUE
   - You can't vote for your own bluff

5. **The Reveal**
   - Correct answer highlighted
   - Show who wrote each fake answer
   - Points awarded

### Scoring
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Guess the correct answer | +2 |
| Someone votes for YOUR bluff | +1 per vote |
| Bluff matches correct answer (accident!) | +3 bonus |

## What You'll Learn

### Knowledge Expansion
- Obscure facts that stick because of the game context
- Learning through curiosity ("Wait, is that really true?")
- Exposure to geography, science, history, nature

### Critical Thinking
- Evaluating what "sounds" true vs. what IS true
- Recognizing patterns in how real facts are structured
- Detecting overly specific or vague language

### Creative Writing
- Crafting convincing fake answers
- Matching tone and style of real facts
- Thinking about what others will find believable

### Social Awareness
- Reading how others react to options
- Understanding what sounds credible to different ages
- Learning to bluff with a straight face

## The Magic Moment

**The Accidental Genius**

Question: "What is the most common bird in the world?"

- Mom writes: "House Sparrow"
- Dad writes: "Pigeon"
- Jake (10) writes: "Red-billed Quelea"
- Emma (8) writes: "Chicken"

The answer is revealed: **Red-billed Quelea**

Everyone: "JAKE KNEW THAT?!"

Jake: "I made it up! I thought it sounded like a real bird!"

He accidentally guessed the correct answer word-for-word. The table erupts.

---

## Why It's Addictive

1. **Anyone can win**: Knowledge helps, but creativity matters more
2. **Pride in bluffing**: Getting someone to vote for YOUR fake is satisfying
3. **Learn something**: Every question reveals a fun fact
4. **No wrong answers**: Bad guesses become funny moments
5. **The detective game**: Analyzing answers for "tells"
6. **Escalating strategy**: You start learning how others bluff
