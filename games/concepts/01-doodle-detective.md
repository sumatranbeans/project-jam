## How to Play

### Setup (15 seconds)
1. All players join via QR code
2. Game randomly assigns roles:
   - **1 Artist** (draws the prompt)
   - **1 Faker** (doesn't know the prompt)
   - **Everyone else** (knows the prompt, guesses to help the Artist)

### Round Flow (2-3 minutes)
1. **Drawing Phase (60 seconds)**
   - Artist draws the secret prompt
   - All players (except Faker) see what the prompt is
   - Everyone submits guesses to seem like they're trying to help
   - Faker must guess convincingly without knowing the answer

2. **Voting Phase (30 seconds)**
   - TV shows all players' avatars
   - Everyone votes on who they think the Faker is
   - Majority vote reveals the accused player

3. **Faker's Last Chance**
   - If caught, the Faker gets ONE guess at what the prompt was
   - If they guess correctly, they still win!

### Scoring
| Outcome | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Faker not caught | Faker: +3, Others: 0 |
| Faker caught, guesses wrong | Catchers: +2 each |
| Faker caught, guesses right | Faker: +2, Catchers: +1 |
| Artist's drawing guessed | Artist: +1 |

## What You'll Learn

### For Kids (6-10)
- **Observation skills**: Watching others' reactions and behavior
- **Drawing under pressure**: Quick visual communication
- **Social reading**: Detecting when someone seems uncertain
- **Vocabulary**: Learning new words through prompts

### For Everyone
- **Deductive reasoning**: Piecing together clues to find the Faker
- **Bluffing**: The Faker learns to act confidently despite uncertainty
- **Risk assessment**: Is it worth accusing someone?
- **Communication**: Guessing in ways that help the Artist without being too obvious

## The Magic Moment

**The Faker's Accidental Genius Guess**

Picture this: The prompt is "ASTRONAUT." The Faker, completely lost, hears someone guess "spaceman?" and another say "helmet?" They panic and shout "SPACE MAN WITH HAT!"â€”which is SO close that everyone thinks they knew all along.

The voting phase erupts: "Wait, they guessed it right away!" / "No, that was TOO specific, they must have known!" / "But they said HAT not HELMET..."

The social chaos of trying to figure out if someone is faking their confusion vs. genuinely confused is GOLD.

---

## Why It's Addictive

1. **Role variety**: Being the Faker is thrilling; being the Detective is satisfying
2. **Paranoia**: "Why did they guess THAT? Do they know or are they faking?"
3. **Redemption**: The Faker's last-chance guess creates drama
4. **Drawing comedy**: Terrible drawings make identifying the Faker harderâ€”everyone is confused!
5. **Accusation drama**: Pointing fingers at each other is inherently fun
6. **Skill ceiling**: You get better at both faking AND detecting over time

## Example Round

**Players:** Mom (Artist), Dad, Emma (8), Jake (10), Grandma (Faker)

**Prompt:** ğŸ¸ GUITAR

1. **Drawing Phase**
   - Mom starts drawing... a weird shape with strings?
   - Dad guesses: "violin?" (He knows it's guitar, strategic misdirect)
   - Emma guesses: "music thing!" (She knows)
   - Jake guesses: "banjo?" (Strategic)
   - Grandma guesses: "uhhh... piano?" (She has NO idea, panicking)

2. **The Tension Builds**
   - Mom adds more detailsâ€”clearly has strings and a neck
   - Grandma sees others focusing on strings: "violin?" (copying Dad)
   - Jake notices Grandma's delayed response: ğŸ¤”

3. **Voting Phase**
   - Dad votes Grandma (her piano guess was suspicious)
   - Emma votes Jake (she thought his confidence was fake)
   - Jake votes Grandma (the delayed copying)
   - Grandma votes Emma ("I'll blend in by voting randomly!")

4. **The Reveal**
   - Grandma is accused! She's revealed as the Faker!
   - **Grandma's last chance:** "Was it... a guitar?"
   - SHE'S RIGHT! She pieced it together from the later guesses!

5. **Scores**
   - Grandma: +2 (caught but guessed correctly)
   - Dad, Jake: +1 each (caught her, but she still got points)
   - Emma: 0 (voted wrong)
   - Mom: +1 (drawing was eventually clear)

**Everyone:** "ONE MORE ROUND!"

## Technical Notes

- Drawing syncs in real-time to TV
- Guesses appear as chat bubbles (anonymized until voting)
- Voting is simultaneous and secret
- 5-second countdown before role reveal builds suspense
- Optional: Replay of drawing at end to see the "art"
