## How to Play

### Team Setup
- Split into 2 teams (Team A vs Team B)
- Minimum 2 players per team
- Teams take turns being Sound Makers and Guessers

### Round Flow (60 seconds per turn)

1. **Sound Maker Sees Prompt**
   - One player from the active team sees a secret word/phrase
   - They have 60 seconds to make their team guess

2. **Sound Making Rules**
   - âœ… Animal noises (barking, meowing, roaring)
   - âœ… Sound effects (whoosh, crash, beep)
   - âœ… Rhythmic sounds (tapping, clapping patterns)
   - âœ… Vocal sounds (screaming, whispering, gasping)
   - âŒ NO actual words (even in other languages)
   - âŒ NO humming recognizable songs
   - âŒ NO pointing at objects

3. **Guessing**
   - Team members type guesses on their phones
   - Correct guess = point earned!
   - Multiple prompts can be solved in 60 seconds

4. **Pass Option**
   - Sound Maker can "Pass" for -1 point penalty
   - New prompt appears

5. **Team Switch**
   - After 60 seconds, other team takes over

### Scoring
| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Correct guess | +1 |
| Pass | -1 |
| Speed bonus (under 10 sec) | +1 |

## Why It's Addictive

1. **Physical comedy**: Watching people make ridiculous sounds is hilarious
2. **Team bonding**: Celebrating successful communication feels great
3. **Creative challenge**: Every prompt is a puzzle with infinite solutions
4. **No wrong answers**: "Bad" sounds often lead to the right guess
5. **Role variety**: Being the Sound Maker vs. Guesser feels different
6. **Speed pressure**: Racing against the clock adds excitement
