## How to Play

### Round Flow (90 seconds)

1. **Matchup Selection (5 sec)**
   - Two players are selected to draw
   - Random or round-robin rotation

2. **Prompt Revealed (5 sec)**
   - Same prompt shown to both artists
   - Audience also sees the prompt

3. **Drawing Phase (30-45 sec)**
   - Both artists draw simultaneously on their phones
   - Drawings sync to TV in split-screen (real-time!)
   - Artists can't see each other's work
   - Timer countdown creates pressure

4. **Reveal & Vote (20 sec)**
   - Drawing stops, final versions displayed
   - Non-artists vote for their favorite
   - Artists cannot vote for themselves

5. **Results**
   - Winner gets +2 points
   - Tie = both get +1 point
   - Special awards for style (below)

### Scoring
| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win the vote | +2 |
| Tie | +1 each |
| "Hilarious Fail" award | +1 bonus |
| "Creative Genius" award | +1 bonus |

## Player Setup

**Players:** 3-6
**Artists per round:** 2
**Voters per round:** Everyone else
**Minimum:** 3 (2 draw, 1 votes)
**Game Length:** Until everyone has drawn 2-3 times

## What You'll Learn

### Artistic Skills
- **Quick sketching**: Capturing essence with limited time
- **Visual communication**: Making ideas readable
- **Creative interpretation**: Different ways to show same concept

### Cognitive Skills
- **Time management**: Prioritizing what to draw first
- **Decision making**: Simple vs. detailed approach
- **Observation**: Learning from how others approach prompts

### Social Skills
- **Graceful losing**: Celebrating others' creativity
- **Appreciation**: Recognizing different artistic styles
- **Fair judgment**: Voting objectively

## The Magic Moment

**The Underdog Victory**

Prompt: "BIRTHDAY PARTY"

Mom's drawing: Detailed cake, balloons, carefully drawn presents, party hats...

Emma (7) draws: A stick figure with a HUGE smile, one balloon, and the word "YAY" in wobbly letters.

Everyone votes. Emma wins 4-1.

Why? "Her drawing just FELT more like a birthday party! Look at that smile!"

Mom laughs. Emma is triumphant. The game proves that feeling matters more than technique.

---

## Why It's Addictive

1. **Head-to-head drama**: Direct competition creates excitement
2. **Split-screen suspense**: Watching both drawings evolve is thrilling
3. **Surprise reveals**: Sometimes the messy one wins!
4. **Rotating matchups**: Everyone gets a turn
5. **Spectator entertainment**: Watching and judging is fun too
6. **Style variety**: Different approaches are all valid
