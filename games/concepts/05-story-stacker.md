## How to Play

### Game Modes

**Mode A: Round Robin Story**
- One story, everyone contributes
- Secret prompts create wild twists
- Vote on best sentence at the end

**Mode B: Parallel Stories (Team)**
- Multiple stories built simultaneously
- Teams compete for funniest story
- Other team votes on winner

### Round Flow (Mode A)

1. **Story Starter (Auto)**
   - Game provides an opening line
   - "Once upon a time..." or situation-based

2. **Secret Prompts Dealt**
   - Each player gets a secret prompt they MUST include
   - Prompts are themed (characters, events, twists)

3. **Building Phase (30 sec each)**
   - Players take turns adding ONE sentence
   - Must incorporate their secret prompt naturally
   - Others try to guess what the prompt was

4. **Continue Until Complete**
   - 4-6 sentences total (depending on player count)
   - Final player must create an ending

5. **Reveal & Vote**
   - TV reads the complete story aloud
   - Secret prompts are revealed
   - Everyone votes on their favorite sentence
   - Points for votes received

## The Magic Moment

**The Accidental Masterpiece**

The story so far:
> "Once upon a time, there was a detective named Susan who only solved crimes on Tuesdays."

Dad's secret prompt: **"Add a natural disaster"**
He writes: "Suddenly, a tornado made entirely of spaghetti destroyed the evidence warehouse."

Emma's secret prompt: **"Someone falls in love"**
She adds: "The tornado fell in love with a hurricane named Harold."

The room is crying with laughter. The spaghetti tornado falling in love was NOT planned, but it's now the greatest story ever told in this household.

## Why It's Addictive

1. **Shared creation**: You're building something together
2. **Unpredictability**: No one knows where the story will go
3. **Prompt reveals**: "THAT'S why you added a penguin!"
4. **Quotable moments**: Stories create family inside jokes
5. **Low pressure**: Bad sentences are often the funniest
6. **Replayability**: Different prompts = different stories
